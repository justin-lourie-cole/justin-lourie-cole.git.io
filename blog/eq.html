What is Emotional Intelligence?

CULTURAL


written by Justin Cole

Nov 23 2020


Some psychologists believe that IQ scores are too narrow and do not encompass the full range of human intelligence and that intelligence may not simply be a single general ability. There may be multiple intelligences and people may have strengths in a number of these areas. Some experts believe that the ability to understand and express emotions can play an equal, if not more important, role in how people fare in life. This is known as EQ or emotional intelligence.


EQ vs IQ


Today, intelligence quotient is calculated by comparing scores against the average scores of people in the same age group. IQ represents such abilities as:


  • Visual and spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning (the brain's ability to take in new information without the benefit of practice or experience)
  • Working memory and short-term memory
  • Quantitative reasoning (the act of understanding mathematical facts and concepts and being able to apply them to real-world scenarios)

“Emotional intelligence refers to a person's ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions. ” EQ represents such abilities as:


  • Identifying emotions
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Controlling one's own emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communications
  • Relating to others

Why is emotional intelligence important?


“No one cares how much you know until they know how much you care.” Theodore Roosevelt


According to the Center for Creative Leadership, 75% of careers are “derailed for reasons related to emotional competencies, including the inability to handle interpersonal problems; unsatisfactory team leadership during times of difficulty or conflict; or inability to adapt to change or elicit trust”. 67% of attributes that employers look for are emotional intelligence competencies. Employees with a manager with high emotional intelligence are four times less likely to leave than those with a manager with low emotional intelligence.


High IQ is no guarantee for success in life. In fact the higher you look on the business ladder, the abilities associated with EQ are far more valuable. As stated earlier, the scope of IQ abilities may be too narrow. It will definitely help you to pass that test you have coming up but won’t do much to help in the wide range of challenges that arise from working with other people.
