Just what is the differences between relative, absolute, and fixed positioning?

TECHNICAL


written by Justin Cole

Nov 14 2020

Admittedly, CSS is definitely one of my weak points. That is why through these technical blogs I intend on making my mastery of CSS the focus. To try and help myself to understand all the different properties and attributes and hopefully illuminate what currently seems to be a frustrating roadblock.


Firstly, I should start by mentioning static positioning. This is the default position attribute each element will have if nothing has been specified. With static positioning each element follows directly after one another in their natural order (case in point, these paragraphs I am typing).



Relative Position


Relative position allows us to make use of the Top, Bottom, Right and Left properties. With our element set to relative position we can now move the element in relation to it's original position, say Top: 10px; and Left: 20px;. This will move the element 10 pixels down from it's top and 20 pixels right of it's left side (confusing, I know).



Absolute Position


Position absolute removes the element from the normal document flow and allows for stacking of elements used with the z-index property. The element is now able to be positioned in relation to it's closet positioned ancestor. If no ancestor has a position property other than default, the element will be positioned in relation to the document body. Similar to position relative, the Top, Bottom, Left and Right properties inform the magnitude.




Fixed Position


Like position absolute, position fixed removes the element from the normal document flow. It is now always fixed in relation to the viewport. Take the soon to be navigation bar at the top of this page. Scrolling does nothing to affect the position of this element. It is always locked in position at the top of the viewport. Once again the Top, Bottom, Left and Right properties can be used to inform where this element will be fixed.

The Story of Te Houtaewa


E kore e mau i a koe, he wae kai pakiaka. A foot accustomed to running over roots makes the speediest runner.

Nov 10 2010

Te Houtaewa was the fastest runner of his day and played many pranks on his peoples enemies. One morning his mother wanted kumara for the hangi (earth oven) and asked Te Houtaewa to go to the gardens at Te Kao, a short distance away. He agreed to fetch the kumara and told his mother to prepare the hangi.



Instead of going to the nearby gardens, Te Houtaewa set off for Ahipara as he wanted to annoy some Te Rarawa people who lived there. Carrying two large baskets for the kumara, he ran like the wind, completing the journey over the hard sands of Te Oneroa a Tohe (Ninety Mile Beach) in the few hours it takes a good hangi to heat up properly. On reaching Ahipara, he went straight to the people's kumara pataka (storehouse for the sweet potatoes) situated at the foot of the hill Whangatauatia.


While Te Houtaewa was filling his baskets with kumara he was spotted stealing the kai (food). He was immediately recognised.


What does it mean to display inline vs inline blocks?

TECHNICAL


written by Justin Cole

Nov 17 2020


Every HTML element has a default display value, depending on what type of element it is. The two default display values are: block and inline.


Display Inline


An inline element does not start on a new line and it only takes up as much width as necessary. Some common inline elements are:


  • <a>
  • <button>
  • <img>

If an element is given the display attribute of inline or is inline by default, the height and width properties will have no effect.


Note: An inline element cannot contain a block-level element!



Display Block


A block-level element always starts on a new line and takes up the full width available (stretches out to the left and right as far as it can). Some common block elements are:


  • <div>
  • <header>
  • <nav>

What is Emotional Intelligence?

CULTURAL


written by Justin Cole

Nov 23 2020


Some psychologists believe that IQ scores are too narrow and do not encompass the full range of human intelligence and that intelligence may not simply be a single general ability. There may be multiple intelligences and people may have strengths in a number of these areas. Some experts believe that the ability to understand and express emotions can play an equal, if not more important, role in how people fare in life. This is known as EQ or emotional intelligence.


EQ vs IQ


Today, intelligence quotient is calculated by comparing scores against the average scores of people in the same age group. IQ represents such abilities as:


  • Visual and spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning (the brain's ability to take in new information without the benefit of practice or experience)
  • Working memory and short-term memory
  • Quantitative reasoning (the act of understanding mathematical facts and concepts and being able to apply them to real-world scenarios)

“Emotional intelligence refers to a person's ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions. ” EQ represents such abilities as:


  • Identifying emotions
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Controlling one's own emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communications
  • Relating to others

Why is emotional intelligence important?


“No one cares how much you know until they know how much you care.” Theodore Roosevelt


According to the Center for Creative Leadership, 75% of careers are “derailed for reasons related to emotional competencies, including the inability to handle interpersonal problems; unsatisfactory team leadership during times of difficulty or conflict; or inability to adapt to change or elicit trust”. 67% of attributes that employers look for are emotional intelligence competencies. Employees with a manager with high emotional intelligence are four times less likely to leave than those with a manager with low emotional intelligence.


High IQ is no guarantee for success in life. In fact the higher you look on the business ladder, the abilities associated with EQ are far more valuable. As stated earlier, the scope of IQ abilities may be too narrow. It will definitely help you to pass that test you have coming up but won’t do much to help in the wide range of challenges that arise from working with other people.
